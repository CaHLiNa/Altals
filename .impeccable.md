## Design Context

### Users
Altals is used by academic writers working in local project folders on desktop, especially users who draft Markdown, LaTeX, and Typst documents and need to stay focused on writing rather than dashboard management. They open a workspace, return to recent drafts, create new documents quickly, and move back into the editor with minimal friction.

### Brand Personality
Calm, precise, native.

The interface should feel quiet and trustworthy rather than expressive or promotional. It should reduce cognitive noise, preserve a sense of craft, and behave like a serious macOS writing tool instead of a web-style product dashboard.

### Aesthetic Direction
Light-mode-first, macOS-native minimalism with restrained surfaces, subtle separators, careful typography, and very limited emphasis. Prefer large quiet typography, sparse controls, translucent panels, and list-based organization over analytics cards, dense badges, or SaaS-style overview blocks.

Anti-reference: generic startup dashboards, metric cards, glass-heavy hero panels, and visually busy landing-page layouts.

### Design Principles
1. Keep writing as the visual center of gravity; secondary information should stay secondary.
2. Prefer fewer, larger layout moves over many decorative components.
3. Use panels only when they improve grouping; otherwise rely on spacing and divider lines.
4. Make actions feel immediate and native, with one clear primary action and quiet secondary paths.
5. Avoid “dashboard” composition patterns for workspace entry surfaces.
